He was not so lucky to be born with a silver spoon in his mouth...he was one of the most brilliant pupils in his class.

Born on November 1, 1980 to the family of Elias and Christian Ukwuaku, Okwudiri Ukwuaku grew up in Nnewi in Anambra State of Nigeria. He is the third and last child of the family. He was not so lucky to be born with silver spoon in his mouth. His parents struggled as much as they could to ensure the kids had at least two square meal a day. Okwudiri and his siblings assisted in that effort by searching, cracking and eating palm nuts as lunch each day. Sometimes, they would go borrowing food from their neighbors and landlady. Not always lucky in that but they stayed strong and determined to survive. Okwudiri, together with his brother, Uchenna and sister, Ifeyinwa, managed to get admitted to one of the most inferior primary schools at that time. He studied at Nwafor Uruagu Central School, Nnewi. While at Nwafor, he was one of the most brilliant pupils in his class. In his elementary six, for the first time, he saw his teacher weeping. His Dad could not afford to pay for his Common Entrance Exams. His teacher lost a pupil who would have made her proud with the result of the exams. That explained her sorrow.

In those days, any pupil who did not take Common Entrance Exam would find it difficult getting admitted to a Government College. As a result, he was admitted to a private commercial college which was very cheap at that time. While in commercial school, he was academically outstanding. He was the Class Prefect from JSS1 to SSS1. In his SSS1 Class, he was made the school Associate Editor of the school magazine called The Venture.

After secondary school, he worked as an Administrative Assistant with Chrisglory Agencies Ltd. He later worked as a teacher in Golden Minds Secondary School, Nnewi. From there, he made some savings which would help him pursue tertiary education. In 2002, he was admitted to the Department of English in Nnamdi Azikiwe University Awka. From his freshman to his penultimate year in the university, he was made the Class Representative of his class. He held the position for 3 years. At his third year, he participated in a heavily contested Students Association presidency and won with a very high margin. He held the position until his final exams in the university. Three months after he was admitted to the university, he became the caretaker committee chairman of the newly established Nigeria Federation of Catholic Students, Ifite Sub-chapter. A year after, he contested and won the presidency of the Association. At the end of his tenure, he was re-elected. In spite of the numerous political commitments while at the university, Okwudiri Ukwuaku still made it to Second Class Honours (Upper Division) otherwise known as 2.1. He was among the 14 students out of over 250 students in his class who got such class of degree. He did his National Youth Service in Babura Secondary School in Jigawa State of Nigeria.

In 2015, Okwudiri Ukwuaku traveled abroad for further academic pursuit. He currently has Graduate Certificate in Project Management, Graduate Diploma in Project Management and Masters Degree in Project Management, all of which are from Curtin University, Australia. He has written and co-authored so many research papers.

Okwudiri Ukwuaku is currently the CEO of Franokwy Ventures Ltd, Matrix Online Marketplace Services and Moonlight P and F International Services Ltd. He is one of the Directors of Unity Standard Company Limited.

He is married to Onyinyechi and they have 2 kids, Chijindu and Asoka (the third is on the way).
