Political Involvement and Working Experience

Ladies and gentlemen, it said that what a man becomes in life could be traced to his/her actions while growing up. His giant strides in politics could be traced to his formative years when stood against injustice and was always fighting for the oppressed.

He cut his political teeth at the University of Nigeria Nsukka through his active participation in Students Union politics. His doggedness in fighting for a good welfare the Nigerian students endeared him to the generality of the Nigerian students. He rose to become the Senate President of the Parliament student union government during undergraduate days.

Upon the completion of his National Youth Service Corps, he keyed into the Transition Programme of the then Head of State Gen. Abdusalami Abubakar that ushered in this present democratic dispensation. He joined the Peoples Democratic Party and by dint of hard work, he became an executive member between 1998 and 1999.

The true test of his political prowess came to the fore when he contested and won the Ndokwa West Constituency seat at the Delta State House of Assemby on PDP ticket in 1999. While in the House, he participated actively and served in various committees. He was also the chairman of the House Services and Protocol Committee in that Second Assembly.

In 2003, he was re-elected to represent Ndokwa West at the Delta State House Assembly and was overwhelmingly elected the Majority Leader of the House throughout the life span of the third Assembly.

As a loyal Party man, he became the Director of Logistics and Strategy of the Peoples Democratic Party that ushered in Dr. Emmanuel Eweta Uduaghan as the Governor of Delta State in 2007.

Hon. Ossai N. Ossai became the 9th Chairman of the Governing Council of College of Education, Agbor. His landmark achievements endeared him to the staff and students alike.

He was also the publicity secretary of the Uduaghan Vision in 2009.

In his quest to serve and affect more positively in the lives of his people, he contested to represent Ndokwa/Ukwuani Federal Constituency of Delta state at the House of Representatives in 2011. Although he was denied victory, his mandate, by the divine mercy of God was restored to him at the Appeal Court in Benin in December, 2011 and was sworn in as a member of the National Assembly in January, 2012.
